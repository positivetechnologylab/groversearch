INTRODUCTION


What is Quantum Computing?

Quantum computing is different from traditional computers in that it processes information differently. In traditional computers, we have bits with values of either 0 or 1 that are used to represent information. Rather than silicon-based chips that traditional computers use, quantum computers rely on subatomic particles such as neutral atoms. Since electron energies are quantized, we can represent values in these quantum bits, or "qubits," based on the energy levels of electrons. These qubits can exhibit superposition and entanglement. These properties allow for parallel processing, making many important algorithms like Grover's search algorithm possible. Quantum computers have the potential to revolutionize a vast array of fields by offering solutions to problems that are not feasible using classical computers.

What is Grover's Search Algorithm?

Grover's search algorithm is an algorithm for finding a given value in an unsorted data set. In traditional computers, searching within an unsorted data set would take on average N/2 tries to find the correct value. Grover's search algorithm is a quantum algorithm that offers quadratic speedup. This is especially important as we collect ever-increasing amounts of data, much of which is unsorted.

What does the future look like for quantum computing?

As researchers continue to refine hardware and algorithms, quantum computers can tackle increasingly complex problems in fields like cryptography, drug discovery, material science, and optimization, revolutionizing industries by solving problems that are currently beyond the reach of classical computers. While practical large-scale quantum computers are still in their infancy, innovation and breakthroughs continue as more research focuses on quantum computing.
